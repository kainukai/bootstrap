Nintendo GameCube
The GameCube is a home video game console developed and released by Nintendo in Japan on September 14, 2001, in North America on November 18, 2001, and in PAL territories in 2002. It is the successor to the Nintendo 64 (1996), and predecessor of the Wii (2006). In the sixth generation of video game consoles, the GameCube competed with Sony's PlayStation 2 and Microsoft's Xbox. Flagship games include Super Smash Bros. Melee, Luigi's Mansion, Super Mario Sunshine, Metroid Prime, Mario Kart: Double Dash, Pikmin, Pikmin 2, The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker, Chibi-Robo!, and Animal Crossing. [wikipedia]

CPU: 32-bit IBM PowerPC 750CXe Gekko @ 486 MHz
Memory: 24 MB of 1T-SRAM @ 324MHz as system RAM
3 MB of embedded 1T-SRAM as video RAM
16 MB of DRAM as I/O buffer RAM
Graphics: ATI Flipper GPU @ 162 MHz with 3MB embedded 1T-SRAM
Sound: Audio processor integrated into Flipper: custom 81 MHz Macronix 16-bit DSP

21.7 Million units.
